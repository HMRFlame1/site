HOW THE CHAMPIONSHIP WORKS

The GameCircuit Championship 2026 features 6 key events. Teams earn Series Points from Open Tournaments to qualify and seed for the Prestige League and future events.

1. KICKOFF TOURNAMENT
Free entry • 16-team Single Elimination • £50 prize pool • 100 Series Points
2. OPEN EVENT #1
£15 entry • 32-team Single Elimination • Bo3 • Prize pool based on sign-ups
3. PRESTIGE LEAGUE SEASON 1
16-team league for top 16 by Series Points • £20 entry to lock spot
4. OPEN EVENT #2
£15 entry • 32-team Single Elimination • Bo3 • Prize pool based on sign-ups
5. OPEN EVENT #3
£15 entry • 32-team Single Elimination • Bo3 • Prize pool based on sign-ups
6. CHAMPIONSHIP FINALS
16-team Pool Play + Double Elimination • Free for top 12 • £20 qualifiers for final spots

Series Points determine seeding for leagues and future events.

COMPETITIVE RULES
2. Player Accounts & Eligibility
  • [2.1] Each player may only compete on one account.
  • [2.2] Account sharing is not allowed.
  • [2.3] All players must be listed on the team roster prior to match start.
  • [2.4] Playing with an unregistered or ineligible player will result in a forfeit loss.
  • [2.5] Players must use the GamerTag linked to their profile or they will forfeit the series.
3. Gamertags
  • [3.1] Players must use the exact PSN gamertag listed on their profile.
  • [3.2] Incorrect, private, or unlisted gamertags are grounds for an automatic forfeit.
  • [3.3] Gamertag changes must be updated on the website before the match begins.
  • [3.4] “Substitute” or alternate accounts are not permitted.
4. Match Scheduling & Time
  • [4.1] Matches must be played at the scheduled match time.
  • [4.2] Teams are given 15 minutes after the scheduled start time to show up and be ready. If a team is not present after 15 minutes, they may be forfeited. Proof is required (messages, lobby screenshots, etc.).
  • [4.3] If both teams fail to show within 15 minutes, the match may be canceled.
  • [4.4] Teams have 5 minutes between maps to start the next map or the next map will be forfeited.
5. No-Shows & Forfeits
  • [5.1] A no-show occurs when a team fails to appear within the allowed time.
  • [5.2] The present team may claim a forfeit with proper proof.
  • [5.3] Any attempt to forfeit teams without justification may result in penalties.
6. Hosting
  • [6.1] The higher-ranked team on the ladder hosts the 1, 3, 5, 7.
  • [6.2] If a host ends the game for no justified reason the given map will be forfeited by the host.
  • [6.3] Host must provide a stable and playable connection.
  • [6.4] Unplayable host may result in a host switch or forfeit at admin discretion.
7. Game Settings
  • [7.1] All matches must be played using the official ladder settings.
  • [7.2] If any settings are incorrect, the opposing team must leave the match and have proof of the incorrect rules. If the team plays the full match out, then the map result will stand. The hosting team will forfeit the map if the rules are proven to be incorrect. If within the first 30 seconds the match is ended and restarted with the correct rules, the match will be restarted from the starting score.
  • [7.3] Playing with incorrect settings without notifying the opponent may result in a map or match forfeit.
  • [7.4] Intentional misuse of settings will be penalized.
8. Maps & Game Modes
  • [8.1] Maps and modes are determined by the ladder ruleset.
  • [8.2] Any deviation from approved maps or modes requires mutual agreement.
  • [8.3] Playing an unapproved map without consent may result in a forfeit.
9. Disconnects & Restarts
  • [9.1] If a disconnect occurs before first blood or before the first 30 seconds, the map may be restarted.
  • [9.2] If a disconnect occurs after significant progress: the team with advantage may choose to continue or accept a restart. Admins will determine the outcome if disputed.
  • [9.3] Intentional disconnects are grounds for forfeiture.
  • [9.4] In the event of a disconnection during a Search & Destroy round, the host is required to end the match at the end of the current round. The round outcome will remain valid, and upon restarting the match, the existing score will carry over.
10. Match Reporting
  • [10.1] Both teams are required to report the match result. If a team does not report within 2 hours the win will be automatically given to the opposing team.
  • [10.2] Reported scores must match the in-game results.
  • [10.3] False reporting will result in penalties or suspension.
  • [10.4] Screenshots are required for proof.
11. Disputes
  • [11.1] Disputes must be opened within 2 hours of match completion.
  • [11.2] All disputes must include sufficient evidence: Screenshots, Video, Xbox messages.
  • [11.3] Incomplete disputes may be closed without action.
  • [11.4] Admin decisions are final.
12. Cheating & Exploits
  • [12.1] Cheating is strictly prohibited, including but not limited to: Modded controllers, Rapid fire, Macros, Scripts, Glitches or exploits, JTAGS, Cronus, Unauthorized third-party devices.
  • [12.2] Any confirmed cheating will result in: Match forfeit, Ladder removal, Possible permanent ban.
13. Sportsmanship
  • [13.1] Players must conduct themselves in a respectful manner.
  • [13.2] Harassment, racism, threats, or excessive trash talk is not allowed.
  • [13.3] Unsportsmanlike conduct may result in warnings, forfeits, or suspension.
14. Admin Rights
  • [14.1] Admins reserve the right to: Enforce forfeits, Adjust results, Penalize teams, Modify rules.
  • [14.2] Ignorance of the rules is not an excuse.
  • [14.3] Any abuse towards admins will not be tolerated and your account may be suspended.
